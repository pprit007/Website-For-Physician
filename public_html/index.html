HEALTHCARE FOR ALL

"THE RIGHT WAY"
The United States spent $3.65 trillion on healthcare in 2018. This amount exceeds the individual GDP of U.K., Brazil, Mexico, Spain, and Canada! Furthermore, America spends about $10,000 per person on healthcare today while other developed countries spend on average $5,000 per capita. Why is the U.S. spending DOUBLE the amount of other countries on healthcare while its citizens are still struggling to afford doctor visits, health insurance, and prescription medications?

In this book, the authors demonstrate that America is wasting $1.2 trillion annually in its current healthcare system which is organized to restrict price transparency in the healthcare industry and is contributing to less time with doctors and increasing healthcare costs for Americans. Furthermore, they offer thought-provoking solutions on how to revolutionize the current system for the betterment of the American people so that they can take ownership of their healthcare, access doctors, afford healthcare, and gain time with their doctors. Start reading to begin taking the first steps towards U.S. healthcare reform!

About the Authors

Dr. Yogesh is an independent practicing physician for 30 years with a keen interest in healthcare economics. While serving patients is his primary passion, he has developed a deep interest on how to improve healthcare for the American people so that it can be accessible and affordable for all citizens. Over the years, he has owned several clinics, employed a number of physicians and nursing staff, and has experienced over 500,000 medical visits. Through processing medical claims for all these services, all the corruption embedded in Americaâ€™s current healthcare system has become obvious. It is his firm belief that the unnecessary regulations, insurance companies, and pharmaceutical lobbies have created huge barriers between a true patient-doctor relationship which are choking this noble spirit and increasing costs to U.S. healthcare. For the healthcare industry to be truly evolved to patient-centered care and to decrease existing fraud, waste, and overutilization, these barriers must be broken and control should be transferred to the patient. It is up to the Genius Patient to learn about the current crisis and rise up to take back control of his or her healthcare.

Niti Yogesh is a certified physician assistant who completed emergency medicine fellowship training at the University of Missouri Hospital after receiving her Masters from Bethel University. Over the 20 years of exposure to medicine, Niti has also developed a great interest in how to refine healthcare for the American people to provide quality, accessibility, and affordability.

Preface

America is the most powerful, prosperous and free nation in the world. She has achieved this status by prioritizing the power and freedom to the people instead of to the government. Americaâ€™s great achievements in science and medicine, highest worker productivity in the world, and wealth creation unprecedented by any nation in the history of civilization are not the result of government intrusion and bureaucracy, but are due to the American people performing at their best. It is the people who innovate and find ways to give maximum satisfaction. That is the wonder of free market economy. Our healthcare institutions and the quality of care they deliver are unmatched anywhere else. It is a fact that our doctors and nurses are the best and most competent and caring group of medical professionals in the world. The problems we face in healthcare today have nothing to do with the expertise and knowledge of our doctors or the compassion of our nursing care since these aspects of our healthcare system are doing exceptionally well. Yet, many Americans continue to face increasing healthcare costs, decreasing face-to-face time with their doctors, unmet healthcare needs, unaffordable medications, and so on.

Why is our healthcare system failing to satisfy needs of patients while the cost is escalating beyond reach for many?

We hope to provide the reader with knowledge and tools necessary to understand the current healthcare system in depth to explain why this is happening. Our goal is to motivate every American to get engaged with the current state of affairs and take ownership of his or her healthcare. Like Mary in the book, it is up to the American people to rise and promote change to simplify the current system and bring our healthcare costs down.
